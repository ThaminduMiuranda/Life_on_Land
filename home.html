Biodiversity and ecosystems

The UN prioritizes protecting ecosystems and halting biodiversity loss through international agreements. This focusses on sustainable forest management, combating desertification, and restoring degraded land. The Convention on Biological Diversity, adopted in 1992, is a key agreement promoting these goals.

Forest

Sustainable Development Goal 15 aims to protect and promote the sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, including forests, to mitigate natural disasters and climate change. Forests are crucial for biodiversity, livelihoods of billions of people, and ecological balance. Investments in forests benefit rural communities and require partnerships across sectors. Initiatives like the International Day of Forests and the International Year of Forests raise awareness about forest conservation. The Rio+20 outcome document stresses sustainable forest management, while Agenda 21 emphasizes combating deforestation, strengthening institutions, enhancing conservation efforts, and improving human capabilities for sustainable forest development.
